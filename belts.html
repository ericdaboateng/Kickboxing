Art of Kickboxing

Ranking


belts are gained through a combination of skills. Technical sets are only one part of the grading but must be repeated each time you grade to show improvement from last time.

How belts are gained

The grading system varies between different dojos. a common order used for grading kickboxing students is: White, Yellow, Orange, Red, Blue, Green, Brown, and Black. Once a student has reached black belt they are able to strive for a high rank black belt.

Training Time. Progressing from a white belt to a black belt can take a long time. I can take up to three to four years to attain a black belt. However, the belt grading system is not officially regulated and standards can differ between kickboxing schools.

Beginner Belts

  • White
  • Yellow
  • Orange

Intermediate Belts

  • Red/Purple
  • Green
  • Blue

Senior Belts

  • Brown
  • Black
  • Beyond Black

what rank are you? Can you display your skills when requested? Do you have the stamina and endurance to sustain your technique? can you display your skill at high speeds with out losing form? can you display these new found skills in a sparring session? these are things you will need to think about when you are going for your belts. The best thing for anyone training is to head over to your dojo/gym and practise
