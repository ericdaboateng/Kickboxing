Art of Kickboxing

Your Health

Health

Better Circulation: Kickboxing classes will get your heart rate going to increase circulation in your body.

Stress Relief: Relieve your stress and anger on a punching bag during a kickboxing class.

Increased Heart Rate: An increased heart rate during kickboxing will help burn fat and calories for you to lose weight.

Physical

Muscle Toning: Kickboxing tones your upper and lower body through punching and kicking the bag. You will feel stronger each time you go.

Increased Flexibility: Before starting, stretch for about 15 minutes to prepare your body to move. Kicking during the class will build flexibility too.

Improve Balance: As you are kicking on one foot, you will build balance and stability to throw harder kicks as time goes on.

Improve Coordination: Each set of punches and kicks pushes you to coordinate the motions between your arms and legs to improve overall coordination.

Full Body Workout: Save time by combining your cardio and resistance workouts during a kickboxing class.

Defend Yourself: Kickboxing is great for general self-defense. You will build self-confidence as you become stronger.

Attributes

Speed and Agility: Being quick and light on your feet is essential for any fighter. You need to be rapidly moving across the ring. That way you have a change to charge at your opponent from places least expected.

Strength and Power: Physical training is one of the most important components of being a good fighter. Dedication and commitment are keys to enhancing your strength. Your attacks will deal more damage the more you have.

Technique: There’s no point in having power if you can’t utilise it with your attacks. A fighter with good technique will always deal more damage more efficiently that one with less technique.

Confidence: We think this is the most important of all the qualities of a good fighter. Like we’ve said before, fighting is not just about physical preparation but also developing mental strength. And, confidence is a key point to good mental training. In order to be confident, you also need to be well prepared for your fight.

Mental calmness: Mental calmness means playing your game with a composed mind. No matter what the outcome of the game so far has been, always be calm and know that there is still a way to get out of it as a winner. Believing in yourself can keep you calm and composed.

The killer instinct: The urge to defeat your opponent can do wonders to your performance. This killer instinct can be instilled in you through anger, fear or mere competitive spirit.

Endurance: Endurance is to be able to withstand any pressure that comes your way. It urges you to stay in the ring for a longer duration. The better you can take a hit, the more likely you will outlast your opponent.

Stamina: Your ability to keep up your attacks and keep moving on your feet will be a big help went lasting long fights and spars.

Attitude: The term is sportsmanship. Yes, martial arts and fighting can be a rather rough sport. But your attitude counts. Good sportsmanship redefines your image as a fighter.

Adaptability: A quality every kickboxer must possess is adaptability. You might have had a different plan in mind, but it might have turned out some other way. At this point, a good fighter would know what to do, to adapt to the situation.
